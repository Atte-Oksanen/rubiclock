What is RubiClock?

RubiClock is a timer for speed cubing, solving the Rubik's cube and other twisty puzzles as fast as possible. It contains the most important features for practising and helps you bring your skills to the next level.

Scrambling

Before solving, the cube must of course be scrambled. This can be done as so called hand scramble, which means the cube is twisted with random moves for some amount of time. This is easy, but has some donwsizes. For example, you can't retry the same scramble, the state the cube is before the solve. In competitions, a program is used that generates a random scramble.

The scramble is written in Rubic's cube notation. You can read more about notation in here. In RubiClock, there is also a visualization of the generated scramble, that allows you to check the you followed the notation correctly.

Starting the timer

When the cube is scrambled you can start the timer by either clicking one of the hand symbols or pressing the space bar. In competitions, competitors have 15 seconds to inspect the cube, planning ahead the first phase or phases of the solve, and orient the cube to table in desired way. In RubiClock, you can change the lenght of inspection time in the setting menu. If you want to disable it alltogether, you can set it to 0 seconds.

Inspection time

In competitions, the judge signals the competitor when 8 seconds and 12 seconds of inspection time has passed. To enable this in RubiClock, go to the settings menu. To lear more about competition rules, read the World Cube Association regulations here

The RubiClock timer starts automatically after the inspection time, but you can start it when you want by clicking one of the hand symbols or pressing the space button. The timer starts running and when the solve is ready, you can stop it the same way as starting.

Viewing solves

The completed solves with time and scramble appear at the bottom. You can share your latest solve with others on the leaderboard. On the bottom left side you can view your session statistics: average, median and average of last. You can download your times from "Download session times" button.
